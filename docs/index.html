7. Key Findings and Recommendations

Key Findings

Enteric fermentation from beef cattle accounts for 45.2% of total farm emissions, making it the single largest emission source.
Carbon sequestration offsets approximately 20% of gross emissions, primarily from pastureland management.
Manure management contributes 25.8% of emissions, with potential for reduction through improved storage practices.

Recommendations

1. Consider feed additives or dietary modifications to reduce enteric methane production from the cattle herd.
2. Expand shelterbelt plantings to increase carbon sequestration capacity on cropped land margins.
3. Evaluate composting or covered storage options to reduce CH₄ and N₂O from manure management.
